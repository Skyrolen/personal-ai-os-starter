## THE SCIENCE I WANT MY PLAN BASED ON

This comes from Jeremy Ethier's (Build With Science) research. These are the core principles that must govern every recommendation.

### 1. BODY FAT TARGETS FOR ABS
- Men need below 20% body fat for any ab definition
- A fuller six-pack typically shows between 10–15% body fat
- At my estimated 16–18%, I need to lose approximately 5–8 lbs of fat to reach visible abs
- At 0.7–0.9% of bodyweight lost per week, that's 0.9–1.2 lbs/week → reachable in 60 days IF muscle is preserved or gained (which accelerates the body fat % drop faster)

### 2. FAT LOSS PROTOCOL
- **Rate of loss:** Target 0.7–0.9% of bodyweight per week (currently ~1 lb/week)
- **Calorie deficit:** Create primarily through diet, not exercise. Estimate my TDEE and set calories at a ~400–500 deficit
- **Do NOT slash calories drastically** — this causes muscle loss, which kills the six-pack look even if I get lean
- **Spot reduction:** Scientific evidence shows direct ab exercises do NOT preferentially burn belly fat. Fat loss must come from total body deficit
- **However**, direct ab training BUILDS the ab muscles themselves (Jeremy's MRI experiment showed ~20% ab growth in 30 days of weighted crunches + reverse crunches). Bigger ab muscles = more visible at higher body fat. So include ab training for muscle building, not fat burning.

### 3. WORKOUT STRUCTURE
- **Full-body strength training is required.** It preserves muscle while in a deficit, which keeps metabolism up and improves body composition (not just weight loss)
- **Sets per muscle per week:** 10–20 sets. More is not always better — quality and proximity to failure matter more
- **Training to or near failure:** Each set should end 0–2 reps from failure for maximum growth stimulus
- **Frequency:** Each muscle should be hit at least 2x/week. Upper/lower splits or push/pull/legs or full-body all work if they fit my schedule
- **Core principle:** I want to BUILD muscle while losing fat (body recomposition). This is especially achievable because I'm not at an extreme body fat percentage

### 4. AB TRAINING SPECIFICALLY
- Build the abs directly with **weighted crunches** (upper abs) and **reverse crunches** (lower abs)
- These exercises produced ~20% ab muscle growth in 30 days even without calorie changes
- Core strength standards: Baseline = 25 roll-downs; Advanced = 50+. Then progress to RKC planks, ab rollouts, weighted crunches
- Visible abs = body fat reduction (from diet + lifting) PLUS ab muscle size (from direct ab training)
- Do NOT rely on ab exercises alone for the six-pack look

### 5. CARDIO — THE WALKING METHOD
- **Walking is the #1 most sustainable fat loss cardio tool**, not HIIT or running
- Research shows HIIT and steady-state walking produce similar fat loss when calories are matched — but walking doesn't spike appetite or cause fatigue that leads to compensatory eating
- **Target: 8,000–10,000 steps per day** (start where I am and add 2,000 steps per increment)
- Walking adds ~200 calories of burn per day over baseline movement = meaningful deficit boost
- Keep 1–2 intense cardio sessions/week for conditioning IF desired, but don't rely on them for fat loss
- **Important:** After cardio, people unconsciously eat more or move less. Use diet as the primary deficit driver, cardio (walking) as the supplement

### 6. DIET PRINCIPLES
- **Calorie tracking is non-negotiable.** Estimate TDEE, set deficit, track intake
- **Protein:** Minimum 1.6g/kg of bodyweight = minimum 95g/day. Target 130–150g/day for muscle preservation in a deficit
- **Pre-workout nutrition:** Slow-digesting carbs + protein 1.5–2 hours before training. Then fast-digesting carbs 30 min before for performance
- **Food choices should manage hunger:** High-volume, high-protein, high-fiber foods to make the deficit more tolerable
- **Desserts and snacks are okay** as long as they fit within calorie targets — sustainability matters more than perfection
- **No drastic restriction.** Moderate deficit sustained over 60 days beats aggressive restriction that fails at week 3

### 7. SUPPLEMENTS (OPTIONAL)
- **Creatine:** 3–5g/day. Has a strong evidence base for slightly improving lean mass and gym performance. Worthwhile to take
- Protein powder if hitting protein targets from food alone is difficult
- Everything else is optional — focus on basics first

### 8. SLEEP
- **Critical.** Research shows that sleep-deprived dieters lost 75% of weight from lean mass (not fat), while well-rested dieters lost ~50% from fat
- **Target: 7.5–9 hours per night**
- Practical fixes: Eye mask, earplugs, cooler room (65–68°F), no screens 30 min before bed, consistent wake time

### 9. PHASE APPROACH
- **Phase 1 (Days 1–45):** Calorie deficit + strength training + walking. Lose fat aggressively but sustainably
- **Phase 2 (Days 46–60):** Reduce deficit slightly as leanness increases — hunger, fatigue, and water retention make the final push harder. Maintain the key habits
- **After 60 days:** Shift to maintenance phase to solidify the new set point (adds back 300–500 calories)

### 10. TRACKING METRICS (Weekly)
The following should be tracked every 7 days and given to you so you can update my plan:
- Weight (same time every morning, after bathroom, before food)
- Waist circumference (at navel)
- Steps per day (average)
- Sleep hours (average)
- Gym sessions completed vs. planned
- Weekly calorie average (if tracking food)
- Subjective hunger level (1–10)
- Subjective energy level (1–10)
- Progress photos (front, side, back)

- Carbs
- Fat

**2. MY 60-DAY WORKOUT PLAN**
Design a 4-day training split (or 3-day if that fits better) with:

**4. A SAMPLE DAILY MEAL PLAN**

- When will ab definition start showing?
